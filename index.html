About

Reinforcement learning, from theory to sustainable mobility.

I am a Postdoctoral Researcher in the School of Computer Science and Statistics at Trinity College Dublin. I earned my Ph.D. in Mathematics from the Indian Institute of Technology Indore, where my research focused on developing efficient reinforcement-learning algorithms with rigorous theoretical guarantees.

My interests span both model-free and model-based reinforcement learning, with a particular emphasis on computationally efficient algorithms supported by rigorous convergence guarantees. My work draws on dynamic programming, reinforcement learning, and multi-agent reinforcement learning — especially two-player zero-sum stochastic games.

At Trinity I contribute to the project Machine Learning for Sustainable Mobility, designing and investigating software platforms for Personalised Mobility as a Service (PMaaS) that use machine learning to improve mobility planning, efficiency, and user experience.

Research

What I work on

Efficient algorithms with guarantees

I work in reinforcement learning, which seeks an optimal policy in stochastic environments. My research centres on efficient algorithms for solving Bellman's optimality equation, and more broadly on improving methods across both model-based and model-free reinforcement learning.

Multi-agent & game-theoretic settings

More recently I have been exploring multi-agent reinforcement learning, with a particular interest in two-player zero-sum stochastic games and their connections to dynamic programming and game-theoretic solution concepts — and in bridging this theory with practical, real-world impact.
